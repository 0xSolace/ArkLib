## 2. Definition — the Shaw operator `𝓢_p`
Let `Wick_r := (2r−1)‼·n^r` (the Gaussian/Lam–Leung baseline) and `E_r^{(q)}(μ_n) := #{(x,y)∈μ_n^{2r} :
Σx ≡ Σy (mod q)}` (the char-`q` additive energy). Define the **Shaw excess sequence**
> `e_r := ( E_r^{(p)}(μ_n) − E_r^{(0)}(μ_n) ) / Wick_r ≥ 0`,
the Wick-normalized char-`p` excess over the char-0 (complex) energy `E_r^{(0)} = Wick_r − slack_r`.

**The Shaw operator** `𝓢_p` is the (nonlinear) advance map on the excess profile, defined by the energy
step-law transfer:
> `𝓢_p : e_r ↦ e_{r+1}`, with linearization (the **Shaw multiplier**) `λ_r := e_{r+1}/e_r` once `e_r > 0`.

The **Shaw spectral radius** is `ρ(𝓢_p) := limsup_{r≤log m} λ_r`. Computed (n=8): char-0 gives `e_r ≡ 0`
(the fixed point); char-`p` below threshold gives `e_r > 0` with `λ_r ≈ 2.7, 1.6, 1.3` (amplifying, then
