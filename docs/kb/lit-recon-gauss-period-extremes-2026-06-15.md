# Literature recon: Gauss-period extreme-value / Cayley-eigenvalue (cleanest-form search), 2026-06-15

Searched the open core in its cleanest forms (sup-norm/house of Gaussian periods; generalized-Paley
Cayley-graph 2nd eigenvalue). Honest result: NO usable lever for M(n) ≤ C√(n log m); one genuinely-fresh
toolkit flagged for future work.

## Fresh toolkit found (NOT a current lever, but novel + worth flagging)
**Habegger, "The Norm of Gaussian Periods" (arXiv:1611.07287)** — proves a case of Myerson's Conjecture
on the asymptotic ABSOLUTE NORM of Gaussian periods, using **Bombieri–Masser–Zannier unlikely
intersections in the algebraic torus + o-minimality (Pila–Wilkie definable-set diophantine
approximation)**. This toolkit (o-minimality / unlikely intersections) is NOT in the #444 dead ledger —
a genuinely new domain. BUT it is NOT a prize lever as-is:
- It bounds the NORM (product of all conjugates), not the HOUSE (max conjugate = M(n)). A norm bound
  gives only a LOWER bound on the house (geometric mean ≤ max), the WRONG direction for the prize.
- It is for arbitrary ODD length periods; the prize is 2-POWER (even) length.
- FUTURE DIRECTION (low feasibility, high novelty): could o-minimal / unlikely-intersection methods be
  adapted to bound the HOUSE (sup-norm) of 2-power Gaussian periods? Unknown; the only fresh untried
  domain surfaced. Myerson's conjecture (norm) is the studied object; the house/sup-norm is unstudied
  by these methods. This is the single recon lead worth a deeper look if the campaign resumes.

## No lever in the eigenvalue direction
Generalized-Paley / multiplicative-subgroup Cayley eigenvalue search returns only symmetric/alternating
-group Cayley results (arXiv:2402.02427 nonnormal, 2302.04022 symmetric-group, 1711.08944 alternating) —
different topic, no explicit 2nd-eigenvalue bound for multiplicative-subgroup Cayley graphs at the
Burgess barrier. The generalized-Paley eigenvalue = M(n) IS the open object (Liu–Zhou); no new bound.

## Net
Confirms (again, now via the cleanest-form / extreme-value literature) that no usable lever for the
prize floor exists. New reading-list additions: Habegger 1611.07287 (Gauss-period norm / o-minimality —
the one fresh toolkit), Myerson's conjecture (Gauss-period norm asymptotics). Both bound the norm, not
the house; the house/sup-norm at 2-power length is the open prize and is unstudied by o-minimal methods.
